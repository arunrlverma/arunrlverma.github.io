Under the hood

Not a black box. The tools cancer researchers actually use.

Your files run end to end through open, peer-reviewed methods — the same building blocks used in academic cancer genomics — with every finding traced back to its source.

Variants

GATK Mutect2, MuSE, and VEP for somatic and germline calls with functional annotation.

Expression & fusions

STAR, Salmon, and Arriba for RNA expression and gene-fusion detection.

Copy number

PureCN and FACETS for tumor purity, ploidy, and copy-number events.

HLA & neoantigens

OptiType and arcasHLA typing with MHCflurry / NetMHCpan binding prediction.

Biomarkers

TMB, MSI/MMR, and immune signals computed with tumor-type-aware thresholds.

Evidence

Clinical-trial, drug, and dependency (DepMap) evidence — with weak directions flagged.

Questions

Before you upload.

Straight answers to what people ask most. If something isn't here, email me directly.

Is this medical advice?

No. Command Center is an educational and research tool — not a medical device, diagnosis, or treatment plan. It's built to help you organize better questions for a qualified clinician. Always review findings with your oncology team before acting.

What files do I need?

Genomic sequencing files from your tumor (and, ideally, a matched normal sample) plus any lab or pathology reports you have. The more complete your data, the more the report can say. You need existing sequencing — Command Center analyzes files, it doesn't generate them.

How long does it take?

Once your files are uploaded, the full pipeline runs automatically end to end. You'll be notified when your report is ready rather than waiting on the page.

Is my data safe?

Your files are encrypted in transit, kept on access-controlled infrastructure, never sold, and used only to run your analysis. You can request deletion anytime. See the privacy policy for details.

What if it doesn't find much?

Some cases carry fewer actionable signals than others. The report still organizes what's present, shows what was checked, and flags gaps and uncertainty — there's no guarantee of a specific finding, and that honesty is the point.

Can my doctor actually use it?

That's the goal. The report is organized around discussable questions with source notes and caveats, so it's ready to bring to an oncologist or second opinion. It supports their judgment — it doesn't replace it.

What about refunds?

Because the analysis runs on your files as soon as you purchase, fees are generally non-refundable once processing begins. Please review the example report first so you know what you're getting.
