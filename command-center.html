Under the hood

Not a chatbot. A genomics pipeline with an AI reasoning layer.

Your data runs through the same open tools cancer researchers use, on GRCh38. Then an AI layer weighs the evidence, separates signal from noise, and writes it in plain language — with every finding tied back to the source it came from.

Variant calling

Somatic mutations from the consensus of two callers — GATK Mutect2 and MuSE — then annotated with VEP. Not a single tool's output.

Expression & fusions

STAR, Salmon & Arriba — what your tumor is actually expressing, and the fusions driving it.

Copy number & structure

PureCN & FACETS — amplifications, deletions, tumor purity, and ploidy.

Immune & neoantigens

OptiType & MHCflurry / NetMHCpan — HLA typing and what the immune system might see.

Drugs & dependencies

DepMap, drug databases & trials — used to generate hypotheses from your tumor's features, not to predict response.

AI synthesis

Evidence-weighted and source-cited — every claim tied to a trial, database entry, or paper your team can check.

Questions

Before you upload.

Straight answers. If something isn't here, email me directly.

Is this medical advice?

No — it's an educational and research tool, not a medical device, diagnosis, or treatment plan. Every finding is meant to be reviewed with your oncology team before you act on it.

Is this just ChatGPT in a wrapper?

No. Your files run through a real genomics pipeline — the same open tools used in cancer research (GATK Mutect2, MuSE, VEP, STAR, MHCflurry, DepMap, and more), with somatic mutations taken from the consensus of two callers, not one. AI is the layer on top that weighs the evidence and writes it in plain language, with every claim tied back to its source.

Does my genomic data get sent to an AI company?

Your sequencing data is analyzed in our own pipeline — it is not handed to a third party to do the genomics. Your data is never sold, shared, or used to train AI models, and you can delete it permanently at any time.

What do I need to upload?

Existing sequencing from your tumor — ideally with matched normal data — plus any lab or pathology reports. Command Center analyzes data you already have; it doesn't generate new sequencing. Most hospitals can release your files on request, and more input means a deeper analysis.

How long does it take?

The pipeline runs automatically once your files are uploaded, and you'll be emailed the moment your report is ready — usually within about a day, depending on how much data you provide.

What if it doesn't find much?

Some cases carry fewer signals than others. The report still organizes what's there, shows what was checked, and flags the gaps — no finding is ever guaranteed, and that honesty is the point.

Can my doctor actually use it?

That's the goal. It's organized around discussable questions with source notes, so it's ready for an oncologist or a second opinion. It supports their judgment — it doesn't replace it.

What about refunds?

If your files can't be processed, you're refunded in full. Once a report is successfully generated the fee is non-refundable, since the analysis has already run — which is exactly why the complete sample report is here to read first.
