Under the hood

Not a chatbot. A genomics pipeline with an AI reasoning layer.

Your data runs through the same open, peer-reviewed tools cancer researchers use. Then an AI layer weighs the evidence, separates signal from noise, and writes it in plain language — with every claim traceable to its source.

Variant calling

GATK Mutect2, MuSE & VEP — somatic and germline mutations, fully annotated.

Expression & fusions

STAR, Salmon & Arriba — what your tumor is actually expressing, and the fusions driving it.

Copy number & structure

PureCN & FACETS — amplifications, deletions, purity, and ploidy.

Immune & neoantigens

OptiType & MHCflurry / NetMHCpan — HLA typing and what the immune system might see.

Drugs & dependencies

DepMap, drug databases & clinical trials — matched to your tumor's features.

AI synthesis

Evidence-weighted, source-cited, and written for you and your oncologist to read together.

Questions

Before you upload.

Straight answers. If something isn't here, email me directly.

Is this medical advice?

No — it's an educational and research tool, not a medical device, diagnosis, or treatment plan. Every finding is meant to be reviewed with your oncology team before you act on it.

Is this just ChatGPT in a wrapper?

No. Your files run through a real genomics pipeline — the same open tools used in cancer research (GATK, VEP, STAR, MHCflurry, DepMap, and more). AI is the layer on top that weighs the evidence and writes it in plain language, with every claim traceable to its source.

What do I need to upload?

Existing sequencing from your tumor — ideally with matched normal data — plus any lab or pathology reports. Command Center analyzes data you already have; it doesn't generate new sequencing. More input means a deeper analysis.

How long does it take?

The pipeline runs automatically once your files are uploaded. You'll be notified when your report is ready, rather than waiting on the page.

What if it doesn't find much?

Some cases carry fewer signals than others. The report still organizes what's there, shows what was checked, and flags the gaps — no finding is ever guaranteed, and that honesty is the point.

Can my doctor actually use it?

That's the goal. It's organized around discussable questions with source notes, so it's ready for an oncologist or a second opinion. It supports their judgment — it doesn't replace it.

What about refunds?

The analysis runs on your files as soon as you purchase, so fees are generally non-refundable. That's exactly why the full sample report is here to read first.
